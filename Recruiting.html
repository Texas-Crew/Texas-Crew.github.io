Frequently Asked Questions

Who can join Texas Crew
Everyone! Texas Crew welcomes all full-time students at UT. While there are some eligibility restrictions for championship races, our team is made of guys, girls, undergrads, and graduate students.

What if I've never rowed before?
That's ok! Most people begin their rowing careers in college, and our program is built around walk-ons. Our coaches will teach you everything you need to know.

When do we practice?
We practice Monday-Friday at 5:15 AM and 6:00 AM on Saturdays. Morning practices allow rowers to attend 8:00 AM classes and maintain jobs.

What is a coxswain?
Coxswains are extensions of the coaches who sit in the boat and motivate the rowers. He or she steers, maintains safety, and executes race strategy.

What is a novice?
Novices are first-year rowers who race against other rowers who are new to the sport. After rowing for a year, novice become varsity members.



The Novice Men participate in an old tradition: "The Coxswain Toss"

Do we get to travel?
All squads race annually in Texas, Louisiana, Tennessee, and Georgia. Varsity rowers have the opportunity to additionally race in Oklahoma City, Boston, and San Diego.

What is the Executive Board?
Texas Crew relies on hired coaches to run practices and prepare training schedules, but the logistics of the program are student-run. The Executive Board handles decision-making and implementation of team policy. It includes the following ten positions: President, VP, Treasurer, Secretary, Historian, Webmaster, Equipment Manager, Apparel Coordinator, Fundraising Chair, and Travel Coordinator.

How much does it cost?
Although dues range from $600-$700 per semester, the Texas Crew Alumni Foundation and Fundraising Chair regularly provide opportunities to lower dues. We will work with you to make it possible for you to row.

Why Join?

Texas Crew offers many things to its athletes: comradery and friendships, intense physical training, and nation-wide travel, to name a few. None, however, are as great as the opportunity to represent the University of Texas at Austin in national and regional regattas, or the competitive drive and immutable spirit so essential to a successful athlete.
