Coaches

Peter Rosberg
Head Coach

Alma Mater: Marietta College

Peter was born in Detroit and began rowing for the Detroit Boat Club in high school. He went on to row four years at Marietta College in Ohio, and to a successful post-collegiate sculling career. He began coaching part-time in high school, and after a shoulder injury ended his competitive sculling career, he began to pursue coaching as a full-time vocation. He has coached several programs, most recently as head coach of Penn State University for the past 6 years. He began coaching at Texas this fall, and has only the highest goals for the program moving forward.

Stephanea Lea Sotcheff
Women's Coach

Alma Mater: University of Texas at Austin

Stephanea Sotcheff graduated from the University of Texas at Austin in December 2013, after having rowed for Texas Crew for four years. During her time on the team she was varsity women’s captain for 2 years as well as a member of the executive board for 2 and a half years. Additionally she has rowed at many nationally recognized regattas such as the Head of the Charles held in Boston every fall and managed to make it to the team’s record board her senior year. She currently works as a support engineer at a local software company and coaches rowing at Texas Rowing Center in her spare time. She loves coaching just about as much as she loves rowing and is excited to get to contribute to the future of the program that made her college experience so extraordinary. Hook ‘em!

Jonathan Amos
Novice Men's Head Coach

Alma Mater: University Of Texas at Austin

Jon Amos began his rowing career as a freshman walk-on at the University of Texas. A testament to the adage that fruitful rowing truly can begin in college, Jon has taken his experience with Texas Crew and directed it into coaching. He now leads both the Junior Development and Recreational programs at Texas Rowing Center and is the Head Novice Coach of Austin Rowing Club’s Masters Women where he is currently training to become a Jr. Boatman. Jon is excited for Texas Crew’s growth in the coming years and hopes he can further the program in its tradition of excellence.

Simon Borchelt
Novice Men's Assistant Coach

Alma Mater: Texas Rowing Center

Growing up surrounded by water sports, Simon comes from a background as a competitive swimmer. He began rowing with coaching from an Olympic rower (his father)! Simon has many achievements as a rower, placing 1st in his pair in at the 2013 U.S Masters Nationals and 2nd place AA 1x at the 2014 Masters Nationals. An all-around nice guy, Simon enjoys coaching people to row at their full potential!
