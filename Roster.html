Coaches

Peter Rosberg
Head Coach

Alma Mater: Marietta College

Peter was born in Detroit and began rowing for the Detroit Boat Club in high school. He went on to row four years at Marietta College in Ohio, and to a successful post-collegiate sculling career. He began coaching part-time in high school, and after a shoulder injury ended his competitive sculling career, he began to pursue coaching as a full-time vocation. He has coached several programs, most recently as head coach of Penn State University for the past 6 years. He began coaching at Texas this fall, and has only the highest goals for the program moving forward.

Tom Janiak
Women's Coach

Alma Mater: Wayne State University

Tom rowed for the Detroit Boat Club while attending high school, becoming the varsity captain during his senior year. While attending Wayne State University he started to assist with the varsity men and lead the novice men’s team for the Detroit Boat Club. While coaching for the Detroit Boat Club he became First Aid/ CPR certified and obtained US Rowing Level II Certification. After two years of successful coaching in Detroit he moved to Austin Texas to begin collegiate level coaching. Now leading the Women’s team, he hopes to initiate team growth and regain the competitiveness of past crews. In addition to Texas Crew, he works at Texas Rowing Center where he is the lead Boatman/ Rigger and runs the Introduction to Rowing program, along with a Special needs rowing program through Austin High.

Colin Heneghan
Novice Men's Coach

Alma Mater: Dartmouth

Colin brings extensive experience to the Novice squad. Colin is an upstate New York resident and rowed for four years as a member of Dartmouth's Lightweight Crew. He was a two time member of the varsity crew in 2011 and 2012, where he medaled at Eastern Sprints, IRA, and was the most improved varsity lightweight oarsman in 2011. Prior to his success at Dartmouth, Colin was a three time member of the First Varsity at Pitsford Crew. In 2007, he won states in the 4+ and attended USRowing Youth Nationals in Cincinnati. Colin coached a small team of junior boys and Adult Learn to Row program in Upstate New York, and he currently coaches at Texas Rowing Center in addition to Texas Crew.

Jonathan Amos
Novice Men's Assistant Coach

Alma Mater: University Of Texas at Austin

Jon Amos began his rowing career as a freshman walk-on at the University of Texas. A testament to the adage that fruitful rowing truly can begin in college, Jon has taken his experience with Texas Crew and directed it into coaching. He now leads both the Junior Development and Recreational programs at Texas Rowing Center and is the Head Novice Coach of Austin Rowing Club’s Masters Women where he is currently training to become a Jr. Boatman. Jon is excited for Texas Crew’s growth in the coming years and hopes he can further the program in its tradition of excellence.
